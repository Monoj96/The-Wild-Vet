Dr Emma Hall

Head Veterinarian

BSc (Hons I) BVSc (Merit)

Dr Emma Hall, also known as ‘The Wild Vet’ is our primary veterinarian and has over 15 years’ experience keeping birds and reptiles. In addition to being experienced treating dogs, cats and exotic pets, she has undergone extensive further education in exotic pet medicine and surgery, and has special interests in surgery and cardiology. Dr Emma graduated with first-class honours from the University of New South Wales with a Bachelor of Science in Biological Sciences and Marine Biology in 2011, before studying a Bachelor of Veterinary Science at the University of Sydney and graduating with merit in 2016. Since then, she has worked at several small animal and exotics clinics around Australia and has been offering a mobile veterinary service since 2018. Emma also has a keen interest in wildlife and spends her spare time tending to injured wildlife and mentoring wildlife carers, leading to her nickname ‘The Wild Vet’. She is a strong animal rescue advocate.

Courtney Buffett Kalman

Practice Manager

Courtney Buffett-Kalman is our practice manager with over 8 years experience in the veterinary industry. She has always had a passion for animals and their welfare. Having 3 dogs, 1 cat, 2 guinea pigs and a lizard herself, she knows how to care for all kinds of animals. She is originally from La Jolla, California but moved here in her teens and fell in love with Australia. Surgery is one of Courtney’s passions and over the years has gained much experience in monitoring anaesthesia on a range of many different kinds of animals from dogs and cats to reptiles and birds. Courtney often has a smile on her face and as a practice manager creates a happy environment for staff as well as for all The Wild Vet clients and animals. She loves all the patients that walk through the door so don’t be alarmed if she goes in for a kiss (with your animal) as soon as she sees them!
